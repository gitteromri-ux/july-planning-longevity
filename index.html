July Planning · The French Atelier Efficiency Intersect

Where leads, calls per lead
and contacted rate intersect.

The French Atelier call-center performance database (843 days) shows one clean relationship: contacted rate falls as you pile more leads onto each shift. Reach is highest at ~20–25 leads per shift, where contacted holds ≥45%; push past that and contact collapses into the 30s and 20s. Longevity runs the same trap — its high-volume days (60–110 leads across 4 reps) crater to 15–28% contacted, while its lighter, hard-worked days hold contact. The fix is to land in the intersect, not to add people. Same fixed team, Mon–Fri, no weekends.

Bottom line of the behaviour

Longevity does not need more reps — it needs to sit on the French Atelier intersect: leads per shift in the low-20s with each lead worked hard enough to hold contacted above ~45%. When it does, its own data already clears the curve. When leads/shift spikes and dialing thins, contacted falls to ~30% — the exact trap the performance DB maps. Land in the zone, contacted volume per shift rises, and sales follow it.

The Bottom Line

The mission is contacted rate, not conversion. The French Atelier performance DB shows the intersect: efficiency lives at ~20–25 leads per shift with contacted held ≥45%, and collapses when leads/shift runs higher. Longevity beats the curve on its light, hard-worked days and falls to ~30% on high-volume days — the same trap the DB maps.

  1. Sit on the intersect: keep leads per shift in the low-20s, where the French Atelier curve peaks.
  2. Protect the busy days: when 60+ leads land, hold dialing depth per lead instead of spreading calls thin.
  3. Hold the floor: push contacted rate to ≥45% per shift — into the efficient band, out of the 20s and 30s.
  4. Then scale leads: via the July plan or a lower-CPL budget — cheaper leads only pay off once leads/shift stays in the zone.
