to the French Atelier Standard.

The goal is more contacted leads per shift. French Atelier reaches ~44% of its leads by putting ~13 calls into every lead. Longevity contacts only ~29% of leads because it averages just ~7 calls per lead — and on busy days that falls to 6. The fix is not effort, it's calls per lead: Longevity's own best days prove that 13+ calls per lead lifts contacted to 88%. Same fixed team, Mon–Fri, no weekends. Sales follow contacted.

The Bottom Line

The mission is contacted rate, not conversion. French Atelier hits ~44% by putting ~13 calls into every lead. Longevity sits at ~29% on ~7 calls per lead — and its own best days prove that 13+ calls per lead delivers 88% contacted.

  1. Set the floor: every lead gets ~13 calls — the French Atelier standard — before it's parked.
  2. Protect the busy days: when 60+ leads land, hold the per-lead standard instead of spreading calls thin.
  3. Target the band: push contacted rate from ~29% toward 40–65% per shift.
  4. Then scale leads: with the July plan or a lower-CPL budget — cheaper leads only pay off once contacted rate holds.
